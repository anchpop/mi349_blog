HTML Advice

10/7/2020 - 3:34p

So I'm basically an HTML expert now. In my long career I've learned many things and would like to share my wisdom with you here.

  • Remember to close your tags. You open it, you close it. No exceptions

  • Math is great. But it has no place on the internet. NEVER say one number is greater or less than another - it will destroy your whole page's html. If you absolutely must, write '&lt;' it will appear as '<'.

    But why are you making a website about math anyway? Have you considered doing literally anything else instead?

  • Don't forget to remind HTML that you're writing HTML by putting <html> at the top and bottom of your HTML. Otherwise it may get confused and think you're writing markup in one of the many other markup languages available for the web. Just kidding, there aren't any!

Some HTML elements are "block-level" by default. This means they start on a new line and take up as much width as it can. Additionally, some elements are considered 'semantic'. These give metadata to the browser about what type of information is contained within.
